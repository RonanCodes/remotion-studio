Andrej Karpathy

Entity · ai-research · Last modified 2026-04-14

Andrej Karpathy is a computer scientist and AI researcher best known for his work on deep learning and computer vision. He was a founding member of [[openai]] where he led research efforts, and later served as Senior Director of AI at Tesla, leading the Autopilot vision team.

Karpathy is widely recognized for his educational contributions to the field, including his Stanford CS231n course on Convolutional Neural Networks and his popular blog posts explaining complex topics like [[attention-mechanisms]] and the concept of an [[llm-operating-system]].

Key Contributions

  • Co-founded [[openai]] and led early research initiatives
  • Built Tesla's Autopilot computer vision pipeline
  • Developed nanoGPT — a minimal [[transformer]] implementation for education
  • Popularized the "LLM OS" mental model for understanding [[gpt-4]] capabilities
  • Created Stanford CS231n, one of the most influential deep learning courses

The LLM Wiki Pattern

Karpathy proposed a pattern for building persistent knowledge bases using LLMs — not through RAG or re-deriving knowledge each query, but as a compounding artifact that gets richer over time. This pattern involves incrementally building interlinked markdown files that capture, connect, and refine knowledge from diverse sources. See [[karpathy-blog]] for the original proposal.

Core Ideas

  • Knowledge should compound, not be re-derived each session
  • Use wikilinks ([[page-name]]) to create a graph of interconnected ideas
  • Each page should trace back to its sources for provenance
  • The wiki becomes more valuable as [[fine-tuning]] and [[rlhf]] concepts are cross-referenced
