Based on your wiki:
→ RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) requires
training a separate reward model on human preferences, then
using PPO to optimize the policy against it.
→ DPO (Direct Preference Optimization) simplifies this by
directly optimizing the policy on preference pairs — no
reward model needed, reducing compute by ~40%.
→ Both require preference data, but DPO's closed-form loss
makes it more stable and easier to implement.
